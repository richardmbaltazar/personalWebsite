Overview

Extending Apple FindMy with safety context

Apple FindMy helps families see where their loved ones are, but it does not explain what is happening around them. For parents of teens and young adults, location alone can still leave unanswered questions: Is my child safe? Is something happening nearby? Should I call them?

Our concept adds a safety layer to FindMy through nearby incident alerts, map-based safety indicators, and quick check-in actions. The goal is to reduce uncertainty without making the experience feel overly invasive.

User Research

What we learned from parents

We interviewed parents of teens and young adults who use location-sharing apps such as FindMy and Life360.

01

Parents check repeatedly when they feel uncertain.

Several parents described checking location apps every 30 minutes, especially when their child is somewhere unfamiliar or not responding.

02

Location lacks real-world context.

A map pin shows where someone is, but not whether there are nearby emergencies, crime reports, protests, or hazards.

03

Parents want reassurance, not constant surveillance.

Parents wanted quick confirmation that their child was okay, while still respecting independence as their children get older.

Pain Points

Current apps leave parents with extra work

01

Parents switch between FindMy, texting, calling, news, and safety apps.

02

Delayed or vague location updates create doubt during stressful moments.

03

Parents cannot easily tell if their child is near or far from an incident.

Design Opportunity

How might we help parents understand the safety of their child’s surroundings?

Safety Map Layer

A toggle that overlays nearby incidents onto the FindMy map.

Smart Incident Alerts

Notifications only when a relevant incident happens near a child’s location.

Quick Check-In

Parents can send a fast “Are you okay?” message from the incident card.
