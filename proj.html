Overview

Extending Apple FindMy with safety context

Apple FindMy already helps families see where loved ones are, but location alone does not explain what is happening around them. For parents of teens and young adults, a dot on a map can still leave questions like: Is my child safe? Is there an emergency nearby? Should I call or text?

Our concept adds a safety layer to FindMy through nearby incident alerts, map-based heat indicators, radius controls, and quick check-in actions. The goal is to reduce uncertainty while keeping the experience familiar, minimal, and respectful of the existing FindMy interface.

User Research

What we learned from parents

We interviewed parents of teens and young adults who use location-sharing apps such as FindMy and Life360 to understand how they currently check on their children’s safety.

01

Parents check repeatedly when they feel uncertain.

Some parents check location frequently, especially when their child is somewhere unfamiliar or not responding right away.

02

Location lacks real-world context.

A map pin shows where someone is, but not whether there are nearby emergencies, hazards, crimes, or other safety concerns.

03

Parents want reassurance, not constant surveillance.

Parents want quick confirmation that their child is okay while still respecting their independence as they get older.

Design Goals

Turning anxiety into useful action

Keep FindMy familiar

New features were placed inside existing FindMy patterns so users do not need to learn a completely new app.

Show safety at a glance

Heat maps and incident pins help parents quickly understand the area around a person instead of switching to another app.

Support quick check-ins

Message actions are placed near the person card so parents can respond quickly without overthinking what to do next.

High-Fidelity Prototypes

Key screens from Milestone 4 and Milestone 5

I selected the screens that best explain the main user flow: noticing a safety concern, checking a person’s location, enabling alerts, and understanding the alert radius.

1. Safety map layer

This screen introduces the safety layer directly on top of the FindMy map. The heat map gives parents passive awareness of nearby incidents without forcing them to open a separate safety app.

I kept the People list visible because parents already use this area to select family members. This makes the safety feature feel like an extension of FindMy instead of a separate experience.

2. Person card with quick actions

This Milestone 5 version improves the person card by moving useful safety actions into the content panel. I added the message section near the top because parent interviews showed that checking location often leads to texting or calling.

The Safety Layer and Incident Alerts cards are intentionally compact. They show important information, such as whether the feature is on and what incident types are being monitored, without overwhelming the native FindMy card design.

3. Incident alert entry point

This screen shows the original Milestone 4 approach where Incident Alerts are added to the existing person information card. The design keeps the familiar Contact, Directions, Notifications, and Settings sections while adding one new safety-focused card.

This placement makes the feature discoverable but not intrusive. Parents can enable alerts when they are ready, instead of being forced into setup immediately.

4. Incident alerts setup

The setup screen lets parents choose an alert radius, heat map behavior, and alert filters. These controls support different comfort levels: some parents may only want emergency alerts, while others may want crime and hazard updates too.

I used grouped sections and simple checkmarks to match iOS-style settings. This helps the feature feel trustworthy and easy to scan during a stressful moment.

5. Radius preview

The radius preview gives parents a visual confirmation of what area is being monitored. This matters because “1 mile” can be hard to understand without seeing it on a real map.

Showing the circle around the person makes the setup feel concrete. Parents leave the flow knowing exactly who is being monitored and what area will trigger an alert.

Prototype Summary

The final flow supports awareness, setup, and action

Together, these screens show how a parent can move from passive awareness to active safety monitoring without leaving FindMy.

Reflection

What I would improve next

The strongest part of this design is that it keeps the feature inside FindMy’s existing mental model. Parents do not need to download a new app, learn a new navigation system, or manually compare their child’s location against another safety map.

In the next iteration, I would test whether parents prefer the Milestone 4 setup flow or the Milestone 5 compact person-card version. I would also refine the alert filters so users understand exactly what each alert type means before turning it on.

Main design value Less uncertainty during safety concerns
Main user benefit Parents can act faster with more context
Next test Compare setup flow vs. compact card design
Future improvement Clearer alert categories and privacy controls
